Problem Statement

1. Safety Detection Using Computer Vision

Expected Outcome:

Develop a system that automatically detects safety hazards (e.g., without proper gear or unsafe behaviors) in real-time through image or video feeds.

Brief:

Develop a system that automatically detects safety hazards (e.g., without proper gear or unsafe behaviors) in real-time through image or video feeds.

2. Propensity to Convert Using Machine Learning

Expected Outcome :

Create a predictive model that identifies which leads or customers are most likely to convert based on historical and behavioral data.

Brief :

Design an ML model to analyze customer behavior and demographics to predict conversion probabilities. This helps marketing teams prioritize high-value leads.

3. Customer Churn Prediction Using Machine Learning

Expected Outcome :

Develop a model to forecast customer churn with high accuracy, enabling proactive retention strategies.

Brief :

Implement machine learning techniques to identify patterns and risk factors leading to customer churn. The aim is to empower businesses with actionable insights to improve retention.

4. Fraud Detection in Banking

Expected Outcome :

Deploy a system that can accurately detect fraudulent transactions in real-time, reducing financial losses and protecting customers.

Brief :

Utilize advanced ML algorithms to analyze transaction data for anomalies. The project should help banks flag potentially fraudulent activities promptly.

5. Sentiment Analysis Using NLP

Expected Outcome :

Create an NLP tool that classifies sentiments (positive, negative, neutral) in customer reviews, social media, or survey responses.

Brief :

Leverage natural language processing to analyze textual data and extract sentiment insights. This can assist businesses in understanding customer emotions and improving products or services.

6. Job Description Creation Using Generative AI

Expected Outcome :

Develop an AI-driven system that automatically generates job descriptions tailored to specific roles and industry requirements.

Brief :

Employ generative AI techniques to craft precise and engaging job descriptions. The solution should streamline the recruitment process and help HR teams attract the right talent.

7. Content Creation for E-Commerce Using Generative AI

Expected Outcome :

Produce an AI system that generates product descriptions, promotional content, and social media posts, enhancing e-commerce engagement.

Brief :

Utilize generative AI to create compelling and optimized content for e-commerce platforms. The tool should help businesses save time and maintain consistent quality in their marketing content.

8. Report Generation for Various Business Units Using Generative AI

Expected Outcome :

Automate the generation of detailed business reports, summarizing key metrics and insights across different departments.

Brief :

Build an AI tool that aggregates data from multiple sources and generates comprehensive reports. The project aims to reduce manual effort and improve decision-making with timely insights.

9. RAG Chat Bots for Various Industry-Specific Requirements

Expected Outcome :

Develop Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) chatbots tailored for specific industries to provide precise, context-aware responses.

Brief :

Create chatbots that integrate retrieval techniques with generative models to offer industry-specific solutions. These bots should enhance customer support and provide accurate information in specialized fields.

10. Review Classification Using AI

Expected Outcome :

Implement an AI solution that classifies reviews into categories (e.g., positive, negative, neutral) and extracts key themes or issues.

Brief :

Design a system that automates the classification of customer reviews and identifies critical trends. The solution helps businesses quickly understand feedback and improve customer satisfaction.

Event Guidelines

The hackathon is open to all college students nationwide. Participants must be currently enrolled in an accredited educational institution.

  • The hackathon will be a 2-day event, not a continuous 24 or 12-hour hackathon.
  • Participants must vacate the venue at night and move to the provided accommodation.
  • Registration opens on March 29th, 2025.
  • Problem statements will be released on March 30th, 2025, and teams will have time to work on their solutions until April 18th, 2025.
  • The hackathon will be conducted offline at the designated venue.
  • Teams must strictly adhere to the timeline and schedule set by the organizing committee.
  • Each participant must register before the deadline to be eligible for the hackathon.
  • A team can have a minimum of 2 and a maximum of 4 members.
  • The registration fee for each team is ₹500.
  • Late entries will not be entertained under any circumstances.
  • Participants must carry their college ID cards or any other valid identification proof.
  • Accommodation is free for all registered participants.
  • Breakfast and dinner will be charged at ₹150 per person per day.
  • Lunch will be provided by the organizers and is organized for 2 days.
  • Participants are expected to use the designated accommodation responsibly; any misconduct may result in disqualification.
  • The hackathon will be conducted in phases, and teams must submit their progress at each checkpoint.
  • Participants must bring their own laptops, chargers, and required materials.
  • Internet access will be provided but must not be used for any unethical activities.
  • Plagiarism and idea theft will lead to immediate disqualification.
  • Any misconduct, abusive language, or violation of event rules will result in expulsion from the hackathon.
  • Participants must be strictly on time for each session, including project presentations and mentoring hours.
  • Teams arriving late may lose valuable mentorship and judging opportunities.
  • Participants are expected to follow the official schedule and respect deadlines.
  • Innovation & Creativity – How unique and original is the solution?
  • Technical Implementation – Quality of code, architecture, and technology used.
  • Impact & Feasibility – How useful and practical is the solution?
  • Presentation & Communication – How well the team presents their idea and demo.
  • Identify Real-World Problems: Focus on challenges that affect communities or industries, ensuring your solution is relevant.
  • User-Centric Approach: Consider the end user’s experience and design a solution that is intuitive and engaging.
  • Sustainability & Scalability: Think about the long-term impact and how your solution can evolve or expand in the future.
  • Rapid Prototyping: Quickly create prototypes to validate ideas and iterate based on feedback.
  • Research & Validation: Ensure that your ideas are backed by data and research to enhance credibility.
  • Incorporate Feedback: Actively seek and integrate feedback from mentors and peers throughout the process.
  • Plan for Execution: Beyond the idea stage, outline a clear plan for implementation, addressing potential challenges and resources required.
  • All participants must maintain a professional and respectful environment.
  • Harassment of any kind will not be tolerated.
  • Teams must submit original work and credit any external sources if used.
  • The organizers reserve the right to modify or update rules as needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions? Our FAQ section has you covered with quick answers to the most common inquiries.

What is Hack Fusion 2024? +

Hack Fusion 2024 is a national-level hackathon organized by the Innotech Committee of the ISE Department and the CSE (DS) Department at Guru Nanak Dev Engineering College (GNDEC), Bidar. It’s a two-day innovation marathon designed to tackle real-world challenges.

Who is eligible to participate? +

The event is open to undergraduate, postgraduate, and Ph.D. scholars, as well as working professionals, regardless of institution or organization affiliation.

How many members can be in a team? +

Each team must consist of 2 to 4 members.

How do I register and submit my idea? +

Registration is completed online through our official form. Once registered, teams must submit a PPT proposal outlining their idea.

What should be included in the idea submission? +

Your PPT proposal should cover the problem statement, proposed solution, technology stack, and implementation plan. Detailed guidelines will be provided upon registration.

What themes can participants choose from? +

Participants can select from themes such as Security & Surveillance, E-Commerce, Generative AI, Smart Automation, and Next-Gen Communication.

What are the event logistics and schedule? +

The offline event will be held at GNDEC, Bidar from April 25–26, 2025. Participants will check in on the evening of April 25, spend the night onsite, and kick off the hackathon the following morning, following a structured timeline for coding, mentorship sessions, and presentations.

What facilities and support will be provided during the event? +

Participants will have access to on-site mentorship sessions, dedicated workspaces, state-of-the-art facilities at GNDEC, and free overnight accommodation, ensuring a focused and collaborative environment throughout the hackathon.
